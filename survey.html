Film Rating Perception Study

This survey examines how the public perceives and experiences the U.S. film rating system. Your responses will contribute to independent research on whether current ratings accurately reflect the experiences of diverse audiences.

Takes approximately 6–8 minutes · No right or wrong answers · Please respond based on your genuine experience.

Informed Consent — Participation is voluntary and anonymous. No personally identifying information is collected. Responses may be used in aggregated, anonymized form for research, publications, and policy advocacy by RateCheck. By proceeding you confirm you are 13 years of age or older. Reference Information — Please read before continuing

The following categories are used by the Motion Picture Association (MPA) to classify U.S. films. Definitions are taken directly from official MPA documentation.

GGeneral audiences. No content likely to offend most parents.
PGParental guidance suggested. Some material may not suit young children.
PG-13Parents strongly cautioned. Some material may be inappropriate for under-13s.
RRestricted. Under 17 requires accompanying parent or adult guardian.
NC-17Adults only. No one 17 and under admitted.
